Garage Shelves

Who couldn't use a little more storage? A few years ago we moved in with family because they had some extra room and we were in-btween places and looking to same some money. They had a 3-stall garage, but only used 1 of the stalls, and we were content to park on the street and use the remainder of the garage to store our things while we didn't have a house or apartment of our own.

I designed these shelves because they were strudy, big, and inexpensive compared to what I could purchase. And, I could build them myself without help.

Tools

Here are the tools you will need:

  • Saw - A chop saw works best, but a table saw or skill saw will work
  • Drill/Driver - You will need this to pre-drill holes and screw in screws
    • #2 Phillips bit (or a bit to match the screws you use
    • 1/8" drill bit. A countersinking bit is even better
  • Tape measure
  • Level or large square

Materials

You can purchase everything you need at Lowe's or Home Depot. In my experience, Lowe's has better grades of smaller-sized lumber that you'll need for this design. Make sure that you take the time to select boards without cracks running down the lengths of the boards.

Braces

The final step is to fit, cut, and install the four 1" × 4" baords as braces.

Make sure it's square and level before you continue!

The shelf will still be a bit flexible at this point. You can use your level or square to check every side and corner to make sure it's level and square, and push it into place carefully as you go.

Once it's level and square, measure and install the braces to lock it into place.

Simply hold each board up, mark where and at what angle it needs to be cut, and then use your saw to make those cuts.

Making sure to pre-drill holes first, place two 1½" screws at each location shown by the arrows.

That's it!

Make sure you attach these to a wall before loading them with stored goods!

You can use the 3" screws to attach them, but make sure you anchor them properly into a stud or sufficiently large drywall anchor.

These shelves are too narrow to use as free-standing shelves, but, if you place two shelving units back-to-back and screw them together, then it is wide enough to be free-standing.
